вқҜ what's IMU used for?                                                          

в—Ҹ From the page I just created (pages/concepts/gps-imu.md), IMU serves three
  roles in this wiki's domain:

  1. Ego-pose estimation (primary use). Fused with GPS into a GNSS/INS solution
  вҖ” IMU provides high-rate (100вҖ“1000 Hz) motion between low-rate GPS fixes and
  bridges GPS dropouts in tunnels / urban canyons. The fused output is the
  ego-vehicle pose (position + orientation over time). See
  pages/concepts/gps-imu.md:24-26.
  2. Ground-truth trajectory in AV datasets. [[kitti]] ships raw GPS/IMU;
  [[nuscenes]] and [[waymo-open-dataset]] expose the fused result as per-frame
  ego-pose. pages/concepts/gps-imu.md:30-33.
  3. Upstream input to neural scene reconstruction. [[3d-gaussian-splatting]]
  and [[neurad]] need accurate per-frame camera poses to converge вҖ” those poses
  ultimately originate from GPS/IMU. Pose error propagates into reconstruction
  quality (blurred Gaussians, failed convergence).
  pages/concepts/gps-imu.md:35-37.

  Not covered by the wiki: direct consumption of IMU by downstream models (e.g.,
   visual-inertial odometry). The page flags this explicitly as an open question
   вҖ” no ingested source addresses synthetic IMU streams for VIO consumers.
  pages/concepts/gps-imu.md:46-48.

вқҜ so IMU is to eatimate the location and moving speed of egos?                  

в—Ҹ Roughly yes, but with two nuances from the page:

  1. IMU doesn't directly measure location or speed. It measures specific force
  (accelerometer) and angular rate (gyroscope) вҖ” pages/concepts/gps-imu.md:14.
  Velocity and position come from integrating those signals once and twice
  respectively; orientation comes from integrating the gyro. That integration is
   why IMU "drifts вҖ” position error grows unbounded without external correction"
   (pages/concepts/gps-imu.md:17).
  2. It's 6-DoF pose, not just location + speed. The output is position and
  orientation (heading, pitch, roll). In AV that full pose is what
  reconstruction methods need, not just where the car is but where it's
  pointing.

  And the reason it's paired with GPS: GPS gives drift-free absolute position
  but is slow (1вҖ“10 Hz) and fails in tunnels/urban canyons; IMU gives fast
  relative motion but drifts. Fused, "GPS anchors IMU drift, and IMU bridges GPS
   dropouts" (pages/concepts/gps-imu.md:24). Neither alone is sufficient for AV
  ego-pose.

вқҜ why position and orientation (heading, pitch, roll) is 6-DoF?                 

в—Ҹ Not in the wiki вҖ” answering from general knowledge.

  "Degrees of freedom" (DoF) = the number of independent parameters needed to
  fully specify a rigid body's configuration in space. For a rigid body like a
  car, you need:

  3 translational DoF вҖ” position in space:
  - x (forward/back)
  - y (left/right)
  - z (up/down)

  3 rotational DoF вҖ” orientation around those axes:
  - roll (rotation around the forward axis вҖ” car leaning left/right)
  - pitch (rotation around the lateral axis вҖ” nose up/down)
  - yaw / heading (rotation around the vertical axis вҖ” car turning left/right)

  3 + 3 = 6 DoF. Any fewer and you can't fully describe where the car is and
  which way it's pointing. Any more would be redundant for a rigid body
  (deformable bodies like a human hand need many more).

  Quick sanity check: a train on a track has only 1 DoF (position along the
  rail). A boat on a flat lake has 3 DoF (x, y, yaw). A drone in open air has
  the full 6.

вқҜ for a car it has 6 DoF?                                                       

в—Ҹ Yes вҖ” for pose purposes, a car is a 6-DoF rigid body. Even though a car mostly
   drives on a 2D road, its body actually moves in all six:

  - x, y вҖ” position on the road
  - z вҖ” up/down (hills, speed bumps, potholes)
  - yaw вҖ” steering/turning
  - pitch вҖ” nose dives under braking, lifts under acceleration
  - roll вҖ” body leans when cornering or on banked roads

  So the ego-pose a GPS/IMU stack estimates is genuinely 6-DoF, and that's also
  what neural reconstruction consumes for the camera pose.

  Caveat вҖ” a different kind of "DoF": in motion planning / control, a car is
  often described as having only 3 DoF (x, y, yaw) with a nonholonomic 
  constraint (you can't strafe sideways вҖ” you have to turn the wheels and drive
  forward). That's a statement about what motions are achievable, not about how
  many numbers it takes to describe the body's orientation in space. Both
  statements are correct; they answer different questions:

  - "How many numbers to describe where the car is and which way it's pointing?"
   вҶ’ 6
  - "How many independent directions can the car instantaneously move in?" вҶ’ 2
  (forward/back + steering), within a 3-DoF planar config space

  For this wiki's domain (reconstruction, sensor sim), the 6-DoF answer is the
  relevant one.
