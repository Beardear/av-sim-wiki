## Key points synthesized in the session

- **IMU measures specific force (accelerometer) and angular rate (gyroscope)**, not directly position or velocity. Velocity and position come from integrating acceleration once and twice; orientation from integrating angular rate. Integration is why IMU drifts — position error grows unbounded without external correction.
- **GPS and IMU are complementary.** GPS gives bounded absolute position at low rate (1–10 Hz) but fails in tunnels / urban canyons / multipath. IMU gives high-rate relative motion (100–1000 Hz) everywhere but drifts. Kalman-filter fusion (classically EKF or error-state KF) anchors IMU drift with GPS updates and bridges GPS dropouts with IMU.
- **GPS/IMU is an input, not a target, for neural reconstruction in this wiki.** [[3d-gaussian-splatting]] and [[neurad]]-style methods consume accurate per-frame camera poses derived from GPS/IMU; pose error propagates into reconstruction quality (blurred Gaussians, failed convergence). No system in the wiki currently *synthesizes* IMU output.
- **A car is a 6-DoF rigid body for pose purposes** — x, y, z, roll, pitch, yaw — because the body actually moves in all six (z from hills / bumps, pitch from braking, roll from cornering). Motion planning uses a different "DoF" meaning: 3-DoF planar configuration (x, y, yaw) with a nonholonomic constraint that reduces instantaneously achievable motion to 2 independent directions. Both framings are correct; they answer different questions.

## Technical details

- IMU: 3-axis accelerometer + 3-axis gyroscope; 9-DoF variants add a 3-axis magnetometer.
- Typical IMU rate: 100–1000 Hz. Typical GPS/GNSS rate: 1–10 Hz.
- GPS noise: meters for consumer receivers; centimeters with RTK / PPP corrections.
- Production AV localization usually layers wheel odometry, HD-map matching, and LiDAR odometry on top of GNSS/INS, so the "pose" exposed by a dataset is a multi-sensor solution, not raw GPS/IMU.
